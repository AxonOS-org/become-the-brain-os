Architecture as gameplay

What the game is teaching

AxonOS is positioned as the operating-system layer between neural hardware and AI. This browser build turns the concept into a strict, honest simulation.

1. Signal stabilization

You hold until the synthetic signal reaches a usable lock window. The gold band dramatizes timing discipline.

2. Intent capture

You select derived intent classes, not raw neural streams. The rule is capability-driven access.

3. Neural permissions

Unsafe raw access requests must be denied. Consent revocation must stop the flow immediately.

4. Artifact rejection

Noise, drift, shock, phantom nodes, and loopbacks are rejected before they contaminate intent.

5. Local-only storage

Scores, XP, badges, settings, and leaderboard remain in localStorage. Nothing is sent anywhere.

6. Non-claim boundary

This is not a BCI, not a medical device, and not the AxonOS kernel. It reads no neural data.
