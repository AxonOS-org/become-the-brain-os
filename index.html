💡 For the curious: AxonOS runs a Notch + band-pass + Kalman chain here. Every microsecond of filtering eats your deadline — and a >±120 µV spike must be rejected, not decoded.

Read the intent classifier · confidence

The cleaned signal is scored against four intents. Each bar is the OS's confidence. Pick what the person meant — a clean signal makes one bar tower over the rest. (Arrow keys work.)

💡 For the curious: a CSP + LDA pipeline; confidence is fixed-point (no floats — bit-identical across chips). Your calibration just made this sharper.

Pass the consent gate capability · neurorights

The intent reached the boundary in time. An app now asks for access. Grant only what it truly needs — your raw brain signal is the most private data there is, and the OS, not the app, decides who sees what.

💡 For the curious: apps hold unforgeable capabilities. No "raw EEG" capability → the bytes never leave the kernel. Privacy as architecture, not a promise.

The four things you just did — for real, this is AxonOS

CLEAN
Signal acquisition. Raw neural signals are filtered the instant they arrive, on a tiny microcontroller.
CLASSIFY
Intent decoding. Cleaned signals become typed intents with a confidence score — calibrated in seconds.
DEADLINE
Hard real-time. Every step is proven to finish inside its deadline — a cursor that lags is a cursor that fails.
CONSENT
Neurorights by design. Apps receive only the intent classes you grant. Your raw thoughts never leave the kernel.
This game is a simulation — it never touches a real brain or any sensor. But the pipeline, the deadline, the confidence score and the consent gate are how the real AxonOS works: an open-source, formally-verified real-time operating system for brain–computer interfaces, written in Rust. We're one engineer building it in the open — stars and curious people genuinely help.
