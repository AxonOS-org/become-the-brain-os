# About `become-the-brain-os`

The community front door to the **AxonOS** project: a free, self-contained
browser game where you play the privacy firewall of a brain–computer interface.

In **Neural Triage** (v17.1.0), packets stream across the brain–app boundary and
you decide, fast: **allow** derived intent through, **block** raw neural data,
artifacts, and revoked consent. The load escalates; three mistakes ends the run.
Let a raw thought slip and it is gone — that is the leak you are trying to avoid.
Finish a run and you can challenge a friend with a link that replays your exact
packet sequence, so scores are directly comparable.

It reads no neural data, uses no sensor, has no backend, and stores only a local
best score on the player's device. It is a simulation that teaches what the real
AxonOS operating-system layer does: real-time decoding under a deadline, deny
raw / allow derived, hard consent gates, and artifact rejection.
